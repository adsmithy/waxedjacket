"A Flint and Tinder Flannel-Lined Waxed Trucker Jacket Review"

There are plenty of jackets out there, but if you’re looking for one that does it all—something rugged, stylish, and built to last—you can’t beat a waxed canvas jacket. It’s the kind of jacket that feels just as at home on a weekend hike as it does grabbing a drink downtown during happy hour. Durable, weather-resistant, and effortlessly cool, it’s the perfect mix of function and style.

What makes it so versatile? For starters, waxed canvas is water-resistant but still breathable. Unlike synthetic rain jackets that can feel stuffy, this one keeps you dry without trapping heat, making it comfortable to wear year-round. And speaking of durability, waxed canvas is tough—really tough. Originally designed for sailors and workers who needed gear that could take a beating, this fabric only gets better with age, developing a patina unique to you.

But it’s not just about performance—it’s about adaptability. A waxed canvas jacket works in almost any setting. Layer it over a T-shirt on crisp fall days, throw on a flannel underneath in winter, or wear it over a sweater when the temperature really drops. The wind-resistant fabric keeps you warm without adding bulk, and the classic design ensures you always look put together.

The best part? This is a jacket that sticks with you for life. Unlike trendy outerwear that wears out or goes out of style, a waxed canvas jacket can be rewaxed and maintained for decades. It’s an investment, but one that pays off every time you throw it on and head out the door.

If you’re only going to own one truly dependable jacket, make it a waxed canvas jacket—because nothing else compares. Based on my own personal experience, I highly recommend the Flint and Tinder Flannel-Lined Waxed Trucker Jacket.

And while i sold off my motorcycle years ago, i'd be willing to bet that the Flint and Tinder Flannel-Lined Waxed Trucker Jacket makes for a better riding jacket than a leather jacket due to playing better with precipitation, and how much easier it is on your wallet.

Sizing

I'm six feet tall and 215 pounds with a 34 inch waist and 45-inch chest. I guess you could say i'm an athletic build, built much like a smaller NFL running back without the athletic ability, of course. For slim-fit jackets, i typically wear an extra-large, and a large for regular fit jackets. The Flint and Tinder Flannel-Lined Waxed Canvas Jacket I own is an extra-large. It wears a tad large, and I think a Large size would've fit better with a t-shirt underneath. For layering a sweatshirt underneath in colder weather, however, the size is perfect. Keep in mind that the jacket's canvas construction will definitely stretch out a bit over time and wear, so size accordingly.
